Norway 3–2 Senegal

What happened
Erling Haaland scored twice as Norway beat Senegal 3–2 in World Cup action yesterday, with goals in the 48th and 58th minutes.
Why it matters
Haaland's two-goal performance signals Norway's attacking depth at the tournament and gives them early momentum in group play. Senegal's late goal from Ismaïla Sarr in the 90th minute proved too little, too late.
The GuyTalk Read
Haaland's finishing looked sharp and clinical—both finishes were the kind of clinical strikes you expect from a striker at his peak. Norway controlled the tempo for much of the match despite Senegal's second-half pressure. This win positions Norway well in their group and shows they can score in volume when it matters.
What to know

Wyndham Clark wins U.S. Open

Purse
$21,500,000 total — winner takes $3,870,000
What happened
Wyndham Clark won the U.S. Open at minus-4, finishing one stroke ahead of Sam Burns and three ahead of Tom Kim.
Why it matters
Clark claimed the U.S. Open title after his previous major victory in 2023, proving he belongs in the conversation with golf's elite. His ability to execute under pressure on a major championship setup—where most fields compress around par—separates the real competitors from the rest.
The GuyTalk Read
Clark's win wasn't the flashiest performance, but that's exactly what wins majors. The U.S. Open setup demands patience and precision, not heroics. Burns hanging close all week shows he's got the game to contend, but Clark's experience and mental toughness in the final moments made the difference. This is his second major, and the confidence from that will carry into the rest of the season.
What to know
  • Clark finished at -4, one stroke ahead of Sam Burns
  • Tom Kim finished third at -1
  • Clark's previous U.S. Open win was in 2023 at LACC
  • U.S. Open total purse: $21,500,000 — winner takes $3,870,000
What to say
"Clark is now two majors deep, and that changes how you think about him—he's not a one-time winner anymore, he's a major champion with the mental game to back it up."

THE GUYTALK READ. F1

Lenovo Austrian Grand Prix — this weekend

When
6/28 - 9:00 AM EDT
What happened
The Lenovo Austrian Grand Prix is scheduled for this weekend at the Red Bull Ring.
Why it matters
The Austrian Grand Prix is one of F1's most intense short circuits, with tight margins and aggressive racing that can shuffle the championship picture in a single afternoon. Drivers who excel in high-speed corners and defend positions aggressively tend to shine here.
The GuyTalk Read
Red Bull Ring racing is always chaotic because the circuit doesn't forgive setup mistakes and overtaking opportunities are limited, which means qualifying becomes everything. Expect hard racing and potential for contact given the narrow track and high speeds. Teams that nail tire management over a single lap will have a huge advantage on Sunday.
What to know
  • Red Bull Ring hosts the Austrian Grand Prix this weekend
  • The circuit is known for aggressive racing and tight margins
  • Qualifying performance typically determines race outcomes due to limited overtaking opportunities
What to say
"Austrian Grand Prix weekends are always violent—the track is so narrow that one mistake in qualifying can cost you three positions, so every lap matters."

THE GUYTALK READ. Tennis

Tom Gentzsch at Wimbledon

What happened
Tom Gentzsch defeated Zdenek Kolar in opening-round action at Wimbledon, while Paul Jubb beat Pierre-Hugues Herbert and Mackenzie McDonald topped Felipe Meligeni Alves.
Why it matters
Early Wimbledon rounds showcase emerging talent and veteran comebacks before the draw tightens. Gentzsch's straight-set victory gets him into the second round of the year's most prestigious grass-court event.
The GuyTalk Read
Wimbledon grass rewards aggressive tennis and clean striking, which means unseeded players like Gentzsch who serve and volley well can make deep runs. The surface is an equalizer—it doesn't always favor top-ranked players. McDonald and Jubb's wins suggest the draw is opening up for tactical players who can execute on grass.

  • Mackenzie McDonald defeated Felipe Meligeni Alves

  • Sports Biz
    Toy Story 5 breaks franchise box office record

    What happened: Toy Story 5 opened with the biggest opening weekend in franchise history, marking a significant turnaround for Disney and Pixar after a stretch of notable underperformance at the box office.

    Why it matters: Disney's animation division has been struggling to find its footing for a few years now. A record-breaking opening proves the core Pixar IP still works when the execution earns the audience's trust — and that has real implications for which projects get greenlit next.

    The GuyTalk Read: Pixar got into trouble by chasing nostalgia without delivering the story to back it up. Toy Story 5 apparently did both. The number signals something studios have been trying to figure out for years: audiences will still show up for sequels, but only when they believe the filmmakers are not wasting their time. That is a meaningful distinction. Expect a wave of Pixar sequel announcements in the next few months — this result just changed the internal math on what gets made.

    • Toy Story 5 recorded the largest opening weekend in the franchise's history
    • Disney and Pixar have faced notable box office challenges in recent years before this release
    • The result signals a recovery for Pixar's animation slate

    What to say: Toy Story 5 just had the biggest opening in franchise history — Pixar figured out that nostalgia only works if you actually have something new to say.

  • Movies
    House of the Dragon season 3 premiere shocks fans

    What happened: House of the Dragon season 3 premiered with a scene involving Aemond and Alicent that immediately took over social media, with actor Ewan Mitchell explaining the moment in a new interview with Entertainment Weekly.

    Why it matters: Game of Thrones universe content still moves the cultural needle when it delivers genuine shock value — and a scene controversial enough to require a cast explanation the next morning is exactly the kind of moment that drives real conversation.

    The GuyTalk Read: The fact that Ewan Mitchell had to go on record explaining the scene tells you it landed the way the writers intended — as something people could not just scroll past. House of the Dragon has been fighting to recapture the appointment-television energy of the original series, and a premiere that generates this kind of immediate reaction is a good sign for the season. Whether the show can sustain it is the real question, but the first episode did its job.

    • House of the Dragon season 3 premiered with a shocking scene involving Aemond and Alicent
    • Actor Ewan Mitchell explained the moment in an interview with Entertainment Weekly following the premiere

    What to say: House of the Dragon season 3 opened with something wild enough that the actor had to go explain it the next morning — worth watching if you fell off after season 2.

    Why it matters: It is the kind of show that rewards actual attention rather than background viewing, and the black-and-white cinematography makes every frame feel deliberate.

    The GuyTalk Read: This is a sit-down show, not a scroll-through show. The visual style forces you to engage in a way that most streaming content does not even attempt anymore.
