Circuit de Monaco · Monte Carlo · The most iconic 3.3 miles in motorsport

Monaco's Here. Max Wants His Crown Back.

◍Circuit de Monaco · Monte Carlo
  • Why it matters: Monaco separates the truly elite drivers from everyone else—one mistake ends your race, no second chances.
  • Championship: Verstappen needs to close his championship gap before the summer break hits, and this narrow street circuit suits precision over raw pace.
  • Watch for: How Verstappen qualifies. Pole position essentially wins Monaco. Everything else is just damage control.
  • What to say: Monaco's the one race where you can't just muscle your way through. It's all about being smooth and not losing your mind for two hours.

Muirfield Village Golf Club · Dublin, Ohio · Host of The Memorial Tournament

Campbell, Coody, Fox deadlocked at Memorial's tough Muirfield Village

◍Muirfield Village Golf Club · Thu, June 3rd at 12:00 AM EDT
  • Why it matters: Muirfield Village is basically the PGA Tour's final exam before the playoffs — winning here means everything for momentum into the FedEx Cup.
  • The angle: The course is playing brutal this week, which means whoever figures out the greens first gets a massive advantage over three guys tied at the top.
  • In the running: Whether Campbell can hold the lead or if Coody's consistency catches up — these two are the real story, not the packed leaderboard behind them.
  • What to say: If Campbell wins this, he's basically locked into the playoffs conversation. If he doesn't, Coody just proved he belongs with the best.

Levels
This week's pick
Levels — one month of continuous glucose monitoring changes how you eat permanently.

Levels puts a sensor on your arm and shows what your blood sugar does in real time for 30 days. The insight most people get in week one: that the "healthy" foods they eat are causing major spikes, and the foods they thought were bad aren't. You'll change two or three things and keep them changed for years. The honest flaw: $199 for the first month is a lot for data you could get cheaper elsewhere — but the visual feedback loop is what actually creates behavior change. Worth one month as an experiment.
