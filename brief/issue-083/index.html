World Cup semifinal: France vs Spain kicks off today

What happened
France and Spain meet in the UEFA World Cup 2026 semifinal today at Dallas Stadium, with a spot in the July 19 final on the line.
Why it matters
This is the most valuable semifinal in tournament history, with the two squads carrying a combined market value of $3.2 billion. Whoever wins advances to the championship match and a chance at lifting the trophy.
The GuyTalk Read
France and Spain are the heavyweight matchup nobody expected to see this late. Both teams have the technical depth and tournament experience to win it all, which is why the market values them so heavily. This isn't a David-versus-Goliath story—it's two Goliaths figuring out which one gets to the final. Expect a tactical battle, not a blowout.
What to know
  • France vs Spain, UEFA World Cup 2026 semifinal, Dallas Stadium, today
  • Combined squad market value: $3.2 billion, most valuable semifinal in tournament history
  • Winner advances to final on July 19
What to say
"France and Spain have a combined $3.2 billion in squad value on the pitch today—that's more talent in one semifinal than most entire tournaments have ever had."

  • Film
    Sam Neill, Gone at 78

    What happened: Sam Neill, the New Zealand actor best known for playing Dr. Alan Grant in the Jurassic Park films, died Monday. His family announced his death without specifying a cause.

    Why it matters: Neill wasn't just the guy from Jurassic Park — he worked across five decades in film and television, from The Piano to Peaky Blinders, and was the kind of actor who made everything he appeared in better without needing top billing.

    The GuyTalk Read: Neill represented a breed of actor that's genuinely rare now: the thoughtful character guy who could anchor a blockbuster without needing his name above the title. He took weird indie projects, showed up in prestige TV, and never seemed to be chasing anything except the work itself. The Jurassic Park role was massive, but it didn't define or trap him — he just kept moving. That's a model most modern actors can't pull off. He was 78 and working until the end.

    • Sam Neill was born in Christchurch, New Zealand in 1947
    • Starred as Dr. Alan Grant in Jurassic Park (1993) and Jurassic Park III (2001)
    • Played Chester Campbell in Peaky Blinders on BBC/Netflix

    What to say: Sam Neill died — Jurassic Park, Peaky Blinders, The Piano. One of those guys who showed up in everything and made it better.

  • Music
    Jay-Z's Yankee Stadium Show Started Three Hours Late

    What happened: Jay-Z addressed a three-hour delay at his Yankee Stadium concert, telling fans he held the show to prevent people from getting trampled. He said: 'I'm really sorry for the inconvenience, but I had to make sure everyone was OK.'

    Why it matters: A three-hour delay at one of the biggest venues in the country is the kind of thing that ends up all over social media — and Jay-Z's explanation, that he was protecting the crowd, is the kind of move that either lands well or gets picked apart depending on who you ask.

    The GuyTalk Read: Jay-Z making a crowd safety call at Yankee Stadium is a defensible decision, but a three-hour wait is a long time to stand around wondering what's happening. The explanation — he didn't want fans getting trampled — is the right thing to say, and it's probably true. Whether the crowd management situation was actually that serious or whether the delay had other contributing factors is the question people will be asking. Either way, it's a story.

    • Jay-Z's Yankee Stadium concert started more than three hours after its scheduled time
    • Jay-Z cited crowd safety concerns, saying he wanted to avoid fans getting trampled
    • Jay-Z's direct quote: 'I'm really sorry for the inconvenience, but I had to make sure everyone was OK'

    What to say: Jay-Z held his Yankee Stadium show three hours because he was worried about crowd safety — either a genuinely good call or a very good excuse, depending on who you ask.

  • Streaming
    Dept. Q — Worth a Watch

    What happened: Danish crime series following a cold case unit in Copenhagen — Scandinavian noir with an odd-couple detective pairing and cases that spiral in directions you don't see coming.

    Why it matters: It sits in that space between Wallander and The Bridge — dark Copenhagen atmosphere, serialized case structure, but it moves faster and doesn't waste your time on filler.

    The GuyTalk Read: If you burned through Borgen or Fauda and you're looking for something that doesn't require a commitment chart, Dept. Q hits. The lead pairing works because they actually antagonize each other in ways that feel real, and the cold case format means each episode has a reason to exist. Solid watch for anyone who likes European procedurals that respect your attention.

    • Danish series set in Copenhagen police cold case division
    • Stars Søren Sætter-Lassen and Laurence Kinlan as lead detectives
    • Available on most major streaming platforms
    • Based on real cold case files from Danish police archives

    What to say: Dept. Q is solid Scandinavian crime — Copenhagen noir that actually moves.
