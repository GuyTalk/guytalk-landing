Markets

Nvidia and Microsoft tank as bond yields spike higher

Tech took the hit this morning—NVDA and MSFT both down over 3%—while the broader market barely flinched. The 10-year yield jumped 0.8% in a day, and that's the real story: bond traders are pricing in stickier inflation ahead of Thursday's CPI print, which means the Fed's probably not cutting rates as soon as Wall Street convinced itself it would.

If CPI comes in hot, you're looking at a late-summer hold on rate cuts. If it softens, tech rebounds hard and the whole narrative flips. Either way, your SPY allocation is fine—it's down 0.7%, which is noise—but don't be shocked if the next 48 hours feel volatile. The VIX wants to move.

Formula 1
Monaco Grand Prix · Circuit de Monaco · 78 laps through the streets of Monte Carlo · The most iconic 3.3 miles in motorsport

Monaco tests if raw speed matters when the wall is inches away.

The Circuit de Monaco has never favored the fastest car — it rewards precision, nerve, and a setup that works in tight quarters. In 2026, with new power unit regulations shifting the grid, Monaco becomes the great equalizer: no straights to exploit your advantage, no room for mistakes. Watch how the season's top teams adapt when they can't outrun the problem.

  • Race:Monaco Grand Prix · Sunday
  • Watch:ESPN / ABC · Race day coverage begins ~9am ET
  • Driver to watch:Bet on the driver with the best qualifying prep this season — Monaco wins are built in practice, not on race day.
  • What to bring up:Monaco hasn't had a dominant favorite win here in years. The circuit punishes overconfidence harder than any track on the calendar — one lock-up at the casino chicane, one overdrive into Tabac, and your race is done.
  • Championship:The drivers chasing points this season will use Monaco as a reality check: raw pace gets you nothing if you can't thread the needle for two hours straight. Consistency and car control separate the contenders from the also-rans. Every position counts when half the field doesn't finish.
