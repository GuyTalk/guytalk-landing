THE GUYTALK READ. World Cup

Argentina 3–1 Switzerland

What happened
Argentina beat Switzerland 3–1 in extra time to stay alive in their World Cup title defense, with Alexis Mac Allister scoring in the 10th minute, Dan Ndoye equalizing in the 67th, and Julián Álvarez and Lautaro Martínez both scoring in extra time to seal it.
Why it matters
Argentina needed this—they were down to their last game in the tournament and couldn't afford another slip. Álvarez's strike in the 112th minute kept them breathing when they were on the edge of elimination.
Big moments
  • Alexis Mac Allister struck first at the 10th minute to give Argentina an early lead
  • Dan Ndoye equalized in the 67th minute to force extra time
  • Julián Álvarez scored in the 112th minute of extra time to break the deadlock and spark Argentina's comeback
The GuyTalk Read
Argentina looked dead in the water at full time. Then Álvarez woke them up with that 112th-minute goal, and suddenly they had momentum back. Lautaro Martínez put the finishing touch on it in stoppage time. This is the kind of game that changes everything in a tournament—when you're supposed to be out and you find a way through. Defending champs stay alive.
What to know

  • Lautaro Martínez sealed it in the 120th+1 minute

    The GuyTalk Read: This is what a legacy act looks like when it still has full cultural gravity. Jay-Z didn't need a comeback narrative or a streaming rollout — he filled Yankee Stadium and brought out Beyoncé, Nas, and Alicia Keys on night one.   • Streaming
    Runner gored in the face at Pamplona bull run

    What happened: One runner was gored in the face and several others narrowly escaped serious injury during a chaotic bull run at Spain's San Fermin festival in Pamplona on Saturday.

    Why it matters: The San Fermin bull run is one of the most watched and most debated annual events in the world — when someone gets gored in the face, it's the kind of visceral story that spreads instantly and gets people arguing about whether the tradition should still exist.

    The GuyTalk Read: Every year Pamplona happens and every year someone gets hurt badly enough to make international news, and every year the debate restarts about whether this is tradition worth preserving or just organized chaos that gets people killed. A runner gored in the face is not a minor injury — that's a life-altering moment for someone who ran into the street voluntarily. The festival draws tens of thousands of people who know exactly what the risk is, which makes the argument genuinely complicated. Either way, it's the kind of story that gets brought up at a bar.

    • One runner was gored in the face during the San Fermin bull run in Pamplona, Spain on Saturday
    • The San Fermin festival is one of Spain's most internationally recognized annual events, made famous in part by Ernest Hemingway
    • Six bulls and accompanying steers charged through crowds of participants during the run

    What to say: Someone got gored in the face at Pamplona yesterday — and thousands of people still lined up to run. Every year, same story.
