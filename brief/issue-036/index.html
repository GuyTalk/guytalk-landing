The Rec
This week's pick
Oura Ring Gen 3 — sleep tracking for guys who won't wear a wristband.

Oura tracks sleep stages, body temperature, and readiness in a ring that looks like jewelry. Battery lasts 5–7 days. The daily readiness score is accurate enough to schedule around — if it says 45%, you probably feel like 45%. The honest trade-off: the ring is $349 upfront plus $6/month for the app, and the heart-rate workout tracking is genuinely inferior to Apple Watch or Garmin. If you want sleep data and don't want a wristband, it's the best option. If you want workout tracking too, get the Whoop instead.

Final Sharp Take

Tech got smacked harder than stocks today—NVDA and MSFT both down over three percent while SPY barely budged—which means the rotation out of mega-cap AI plays is real. Bitcoin's already down five percent, which tells you institutional money isn't just trimming positions, it's walking away from risk entirely. The World Cup opens in eight days and nobody's thinking about summer portfolios yet. This is a get-defensive week, not a hunt-for-bargains week.
