Final Sharp Take

Baseball went full blowout today—Tigers, White Sox, and Mets all won by five runs or more, which means someone's pitching staff got completely shredded and the other team's just ran away with it. Nothing sexy about that, but it does tell you the margin for error is gone heading into a World Cup month where every game matters and every miss costs you. Meanwhile your tech stocks got hammered again (NVDA down 3.6, MSFT down 3.2) while bonds climbed, which is the classic flight-to-safety move right before something bigger shifts. Knicks-Spurs Game 2 tomorrow night is going to feel small by comparison, but at least you know someone's actually playing for something.
