Victor Wembanyama dropped 29 points and dominated inside, but the Knicks' balanced attack—with Karl-Anthony Towns, Mikal Bridges, and Jalen Brunson all chipping in 20 points—was too much to overcome. New York stole Game 2 by one point to go up 2-0 in the Finals.

  • Why it matters: Wembanyama proved he can carry a team offensively, but the Knicks exposed the Spurs' depth problem: when one guy goes for 29, nobody else steps up enough to win close games.
  • The other angle: The Knicks' three-guard setup is working. Brunson and Bridges ran the offense while Towns cleaned up on the boards (13 rebounds), and that flexibility is hard to defend in a seven-game series.
  • What to say: Wembanyama's a monster, but the Knicks have four guys who can beat you on any given night. San Antonio needs De'Aaron Fox and someone else to step up, or this gets ugly fast.
