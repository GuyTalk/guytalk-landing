# GuyTalk: Sports

The Spurs are back. Tim Duncan closed out Oklahoma City 111–103 in Game 7, a masterclass in half-court execution that reminded everyone why San Antonio's been the West's most reliable operator for two decades. This sets up a Finals matchup against whoever emerges from the East—and Duncan's proven he can still impose his will on any big man on the planet.

**Conversation starter:** Duncan's +18 in the deciding game at age 38—that's not nostalgia, that's just dominance on a schedule.

  • Key number: Tony Parker hit 6 of 9 threes and finished with 27 points. OKC's perimeter defense collapsed in the fourth quarter.
  • Series: Spurs advance to the Finals with a 4-3 series win. San Antonio survives a Game 7 grinder in Oklahoma City and heads to face Miami (or LeBron's team) starting June 6th. This was the kind of series that ages you—five games decided by four points or fewer.
  • How to watch: West Finals · Game 7 · Chesapeake Energy Arena, OKC · Final (completed) · TNT broadcast.

Culture
  • Nick Bilton Takes Over '60 Minutes' After Newsroom Purge
    The Washington Post · Media

    Nick Bilton, Vanity Fair's former tech columnist and author, is stepping in to stabilize 60 Minutes after a wave of on-air and behind-the-scenes firings gutted the legendary broadcast. The shake-up followed the Trump-Marty Baron interview debacle and internal credibility issues that made the show a punchline. CBS is betting Bilton can restore rigor without the arrogance that sank the segment's reputation. Ask your guy friends: has anyone actually watched 60 Minutes in the last three years, or does it just feel like it exists?

  • NCAA Moves to Banish Brendan Sorsby Over Tampering, Portal Rules
    NBC Sports · College Football

    The NCAA filed charges to suspend Cincinnati quarterback Brendan Sorsby, alleging violations tied to recruiting contact and portal tampering — a rare enforcement move in the transfer era where the NCAA has looked toothless. Sorsby transferred from Cincinnati to Louisville last offseason and threw for 3,400 yards with 25 TDs this season, making his potential banishment a statement play. The case signals the NCAA finally drawing a line on how schools can contact portal players, though enforcement this late in the season feels vindictive rather than preventative. If the NCAA actually sticks this one, it changes how programs approach next year's portal window.

  • This weekend: 'Didi' on Netflix
    Netflix · Documentary

    Sean Penn directs this intimate profile of his friend Didi, a legendary musician and cultural figure, exploring legacy and friendship in real time. It's less concert doc, more conversation between two men trying to make sense of what lasts. Worth watching if you prefer substance to spectacle.

The Sharp Take

The Spurs just reminded everyone what championship DNA looks like. San Antonio won Game 7 at Oklahoma City without their best player healthy, with Gregg Popovich managing the pace like a chess grandmaster, and their role players executing in crunch time. This wasn't flashy. It was suffocating. The Thunder outshot them and still lost because the Spurs held OKC to 103 points in a Game 7—that's not luck, that's system. Popovich's final act in the West was a masterclass in the margins.

The rest of the weekend played it safe. Markets treading water, golf heating up with Henley in front, and Hollywood doing what it does: celebrating its own while pretending the industry isn't still figuring out what it is. Nothing moved the needle because nothing had to. Tell someone the Spurs closed out the Thunder in seven and watch them actually care about one series.

If you've got a text chain about the playoffs, Popovich's management of OKC's pace is the move. Everyone will have the final score. You'll have the why.
