The Pirates just beat Houston 10-6, and the Astros are about to learn what every team learns when the Pirates show up: they're built different this year. Cleveland demolished the Yankees 9-4, which is the kind of blowout that doesn't happen in June unless one team is genuinely better. Milwaukee handled San Francisco 8-3.

What you're actually seeing here is the gap between regular season performance and playoff readiness. Houston came in favored. Pittsburgh walked in and reminded them that ranking doesn't equal results.

The Finals tip off tonight — Knicks at Spurs in San Antonio. This is the moment everything narrows down: two teams, elimination basketball, and seven games to settle it. The Spurs have built something special defensively, but the Knicks have experience in these spots that San Antonio just doesn't have. Watch whether Thibodeau goes small in the first quarter — it's the one adjustment that could neutralize Wembanyama's gravity on pick-and-rolls if the Knicks decide to attack early and force San Antonio to commit defenders.

Memorial Tournament at E is Campbell's to lose, which means nothing until Friday. Monaco is coming up on the F1 calendar, and the World Cup is nine days out — when the real tournament starts, everything before this gets forgotten fast.

What to say tonight if someone asks about the Finals: "The Spurs defense is real, but the Knicks have seen tighter pressure in the playoffs before. We're about to find out if experience beats talent." That's not a guess — it's just watching who wants it more when the clock stops for nobody.

  • Key number: 10-6. Pirates scored in bunches — that's not sustainable against Houston, but it happened tonight.
  • Series: Pittsburgh picked up a road win in a division matchup. Houston's still in the thick of it, but a 4-run loss at home stings when you're fighting for positioning. Neither team's season hinges on this, but the Pirates showed they can compete in someone else's park.
  • How to watch: Check ESPN for the next game schedule.

Monaco Grand Prix · Circuit de Monaco · 78 laps through the streets of Monte Carlo · The most iconic 3.3 miles in motorsport

Monaco rewards precision. This year it demands perfection.

The Circuit de Monaco hasn't changed, but F1 has — cars are faster, margins thinner, and one mistake ends your race in the barriers. For drivers chasing the championship, this is where you separate yourself or hand points to rivals. Street circuits punish hesitation.

  • Race:Monaco Grand Prix · Sunday
  • Watch:ESPN / ABC · Race day coverage begins ~9am ET
  • Driver to watch:Go with whoever's sitting third or fourth in the grid — they'll have fresh tires for the restart, cleaner air, and less to lose. Monaco rewards the patient aggressor, not the guy defending.
  • What to bring up:Monaco is the only race where qualifying basically decides the grid for Sunday. Pole position here isn't an advantage — it's the race. The driver who nails Saturday afternoon wins 80% of the time.

The Sharp Take

The Knicks are down 0-1 to San Antonio tonight, and everyone's treating it like a referendum on New York's depth. Here's what's actually happening: the Spurs are playing the kind of suffocating defense that makes your offense look broken, even when it isn't. San Antonio forced turnovers, controlled the glass, and made every possession feel like a half-court grind. The Knicks didn't lose because they're not good enough. They lost because the Spurs are built to make you earn every single point — and New York hasn't played 48 minutes of that basketball in the regular season.

This series flips if the Knicks can solve the Spurs' length in the paint and cut turnovers in half. Game 2 is the gut-check: either New York adjusts and steals one in San Antonio, or they're staring down a 0-2 hole with momentum fully against them. Watch whether the Knicks' ball movement actually flows or whether they're still hunting for loose space against that defense.
