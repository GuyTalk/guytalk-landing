Formula 1
Monaco Grand Prix · Circuit de Monaco · 78 laps through the streets of Monte Carlo · The most iconic 3.3 miles in motorsport

Monaco rewards precision. Verstappen needs it more than ever.

Verstappen arrives at the only circuit where raw pace matters less than nerve. The championship picture has tightened since Miami, and Monaco's zero-margin-for-error layout punishes the team that makes the first mistake. Red Bull's setup advantage evaporates on these narrow streets.

  • Race:Monaco Grand Prix · Sunday
  • Watch:ESPN / ABC · Race day coverage begins ~9am ET
  • Driver to watch:Bet on the driver with the steadiest hands under pressure who isn't named Verstappen—someone who thrives when the car feels undrivable and mistakes end careers.
  • What to bring up:Monaco has produced only two lead changes in the last six years. Qualifying determines 80 percent of the grid order at the finish line.
  • Championship:Verstappen holds the points lead, but the gap to the second-place challenger has compressed. A crash or poor qualifying here erases weeks of advantage in a championship measured by inches.

The Sharp Take

Milwaukee just beat San Francisco 16-2, which looks like a blowout until you realize it's June and the Brewers are reminding everyone that run differential in baseball doesn't care about narrative. Detroit also won a one-run game against Tampa, and Colorado took down the Angels by one—three AL West/Central games decided by a combined two runs, the kind of churn that sorts actual contenders from pretenders. The Brewers' 14-run margin is an outlier, but the real story is that the middle of June is when teams that built rosters right start separating from the field.

Baseball's second month is when you stop thinking about Opening Day projections and start watching who actually hits in leverage situations. Tomorrow night the Spurs host the Knicks in Game 1 of the NBA Finals—a completely different sport, but same principle applies: roster construction and depth show up now.
