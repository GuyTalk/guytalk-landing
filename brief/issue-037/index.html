Jalen Brunson dropped 30 points to lift New York past San Antonio 105–95 in the NBA Finals opener. The Knicks' offense ran through Brunson all night while Victor Wembanyama countered with 26 points and 12 rebounds, but it wasn't enough to keep pace.

  • Why it matters: New York's depth showed up—Karl-Anthony Towns added 18 and 12 boards, OG Anunoby chipped in 17—while San Antonio's supporting cast around Wembanyama couldn't generate enough offense to match.
  • The other angle: Brunson's ability to control the pace and create for others (only 2 assists though) suggests the Knicks' guard play will be the deciding factor in this series.
  • What to say: Brunson's 30 was efficient and he looked in control the whole way. Wembanyama's a problem on the glass and defensively, but the Knicks have too many scorers for one guy to cover. Game 2 tomorrow in San Antonio gets interesting if the Spurs can tighten up their bench scoring.
