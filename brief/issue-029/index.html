Formula 1
Monaco Grand Prix · Circuit de Monaco · 78 laps through the streets of Monte Carlo · The most iconic 3.3 miles in motorsport

Monaco demands perfection. Verstappen's about to prove why.

The Circuit de Monaco isn't just tight—it's a championship filter. One mistake ends your weekend, and with the 2026 grid this compressed, whoever adapts fastest to the bumps and wall proximity wins the narrative. Verstappen's been here before. Most of this grid hasn't.

  • Race:Monaco Grand Prix · Sunday
  • Watch:ESPN / ABC · Race day coverage begins ~9am ET
  • Driver to watch:Bet on whoever qualified third or fourth. They'll have fresher tires and nothing to lose, which is exactly what Monaco rewards.
  • What to bring up:Monaco's the only race where qualifying matters more than race day. The grid is set Friday, and passing is a myth. If you don't nail Saturday, you're watching from behind for two hours.
  • Championship:The 2026 title fight comes down to consistency and nerve. Verstappen leads the way, but Monaco separates the driven from the desperate—one lock-up, one misjudged apex, and a championship contender becomes a spectator.

The Sharp Take

Milwaukee just did what the 2024 Dodgers couldn't — it stomped someone in June and made it look routine. Sixteen runs on the Giants is the kind of statement that doesn't need explaining to your team. The real story isn't the score; it's that the Brewers are built to sustain this. They're not a hot streak. They're a team that can bury you in the middle of the season and still have gas left for October.

The NBA Finals tip off tomorrow night in San Antonio, and here's what matters: New York and the Spurs (who haven't won a championship since 2014, by the way) are about to show whether regular-season dominance actually translates when the lights get brightest. Watch the first quarter — that's when you'll know if the Knicks are rattled or ready. If they're loose, this could get ugly fast.
