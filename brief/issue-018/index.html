  • Jay-Z Goes Nuclear on Ye, Drake, and Nicki at Roots Picnic
    Entertainment Weekly · Hip-Hop

    Jay-Z freestyled at the Roots Picnic in Philadelphia over the weekend and spent significant time dismantling Ye, Drake, and Nicki Minaj—a rare public move for someone who usually lets his catalog speak. The freestyle was sharp, specific, and clearly months of frustration unspooled live. This matters because Jay-Z doesn't do reactive beef anymore; when he does, people listen and the industry remembers. The man basically confirmed what everyone already knew: he's not interested in the noise, only in being right.

  • Chicago Bears Stadium Bill Hinges on Public Ownership—Here's What Actually Changes
    Chicago Sun-Times · Politics

    Illinois lawmakers are trying to save the Bears stadium deal by forcing public ownership of the building, a fourth-down desperation move that mirrors what other cities did with their NFL venues. The structure flips the usual private-billionaire playbook: fans get a stake, the city gets control, and the McCaskey family still gets their team. This is the actual fix for stadium giveaways, and Chicago nearly botched it twice before figuring it out. Watch this model—it's the only one that doesn't require cities to mortgage schools and fire departments.

  • This weekend: Didi
    Netflix · Documentary

    Sean Baker's documentary about Didi Conn, the actress from Grease and Shampoo who spent decades getting typecast and then reclaimed her career on her own terms, is a tight 90-minute portrait of someone who refused the usual retirement script. Baker doesn't sentimentalize it; he just lets her talk. It's a story about longevity in an industry built on disposability, and it lands differently if you've watched anyone you cared about get aged out of the room.
