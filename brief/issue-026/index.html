Formula 1
Monaco Grand Prix · Circuit de Monaco · The most iconic race on the calendar

Monaco demands perfection. Verstappen can't afford mistakes.

Monaco is the one track where raw speed doesn't matter — it's about nerve, precision, and not punting yourself into the barriers on lap one. Verstappen's championship lead gets tested here because this circuit punishes aggression and rewards calculated risk, which is almost the opposite of how he's built his season.

  • Race:Monaco Grand Prix · Sunday
  • Watch:ESPN / ABC · Race day coverage begins ~9am ET
  • Driver to watch:Bet on someone hungrier than the favorites — Monaco rewards the driver who's got something to prove and nothing to lose in the first lap, which usually means a team-mate or a guy sitting third in points who's ready to take a calculated swing.
  • What to bring up:Monaco has zero margin for error. One lock-up, one half-meter mistake, and you're in the wall watching from the paddock. No safety car saves you here — it just means everyone else gets a free pit window.
  • Championship:Verstappen carries the lead into Monaco, but this is the kind of circuit where a rival can close the gap fast if Max gets caught out. The championship isn't decided here, but a bad weekend stings differently at a place where you can't make up time on tire strategy or setup alone.
