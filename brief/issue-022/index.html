Monaco tests nerve and machinery in tight streets.

The Circuit de Monaco eliminates margin for error—one mistake ends your race in the barriers. This is where raw skill and car control matter more than horsepower, making it unpredictable compared to power circuits where the fastest car usually wins.

The Sharp Take

Russell Henley won the Charles Schwab Challenge at 13-under, picking up his second PGA Tour victory of the season and proving that consistency—not flash—still moves the needle in golf. The field at Colonial was predictable and stacked, yet Henley's name isn't one that moves markets or social feeds, which is exactly the point: he's doing the work nobody glamorizes anymore. In a sport obsessed with distance and youth, a 32-year-old grinding out back-to-back tour wins reminds you that the guys who show up every week still own the leaderboard.

The NBA Finals tip off tomorrow night in San Antonio with the Knicks traveling to face the Spurs, and this matchup is a referendum on whether New York's older roster can survive a grind series against a well-coached, efficient team. The Spurs have already sent the Celtics and Nuggets home, so the Knicks aren't walking into a trap—they're walking into the hardest Finals matchup they could've drawn. Your move: if you're a Knicks fan, don't watch game recaps until tipoff tomorrow; if you're anything else, clear your evening for 8:30 ET and watch a Finals series that will actually test pace and spacing.
