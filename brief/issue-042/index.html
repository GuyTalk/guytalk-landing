Victor Wembanyama dropped 32 points and ran the offense with 6 assists while Jalen Brunson matched him point-for-point, but San Antonio's depth—Stephon Castle added 23 and Dylan Harper grabbed 9 boards—was enough to steal Game 3 at home. New York still leads the series 2-1, but the Spurs just proved they can win without needing a blowout.

  • Why it matters: The Knicks have an answer for Wembanyama's individual dominance in OG Anunoby (28 points), but they can't match San Antonio's secondary scoring when Castle and Harper get rolling.
  • The other angle: Game 4 is tomorrow in New York, which means the Spurs have to survive hostile territory or they're headed home down 3-1 with no real margin for error.
  • What to say: Brunson and Wembanyama basically canceled each other out, so this one came down to Josh Hart and Dylan Harper—and Harper's the one who showed up on the glass.
