Victor Wembanyama and Jalen Brunson both dropped 32 points in Game 3, but San Antonio's depth made the difference. The Spurs beat New York 115-111 to cut the series to 2-1, with Stephon Castle adding 23 points and 5 assists and Dylan Harper chipping in 13 points and 9 rebounds off the bench while the Knicks leaned almost entirely on their two stars.

  • Why it matters: New York's supporting cast got exposed. OG Anunoby put up 28, but Josh Hart's 16 points was the only other real contribution, and that is a thin margin when San Antonio had four guys in double figures and Wembanyama dishing 6 assists on top of his 32.
  • The other angle: San Antonio proved this series has real life left in it. The Knicks looked like they were cruising toward a close-out with a 2-0 lead, but Castle's playmaking and Wembanyama's two-way dominance flipped the script in one game on the road.
  • What to say: Brunson played great and still lost. The Knicks' problem isn't their star — it's that Wembanyama had Castle, Harper, and real help, and New York basically ran a two-man show.

Golf

RBC Canadian Open tees off — FedEx Cup race heats up.

  • Why you should care: This is a full-field event that counts toward the FedEx Cup playoffs, meaning players who have had slow starts to the season can still move the needle on their standing before the postseason push begins.
  • Course angle: No leaderboard yet, so the field is wide open — this is the kind of week where an under-the-radar player can make a run and suddenly become relevant in the FedEx Cup conversation.
  • Watch for: Which players near the FedEx Cup bubble use this event to climb, and whether any of the top names show the kind of form that signals they are peaking at the right time.
  • What to say: Canadian Open is the tournament that sneaks up on you every year — nobody's talking about it Monday, and then by Sunday someone's making a run that actually matters for the playoffs.
Formula 1
Monaco Grand Prix · Circuit de Monaco · 78 laps through the streets of Monte Carlo · The most iconic 3.3 miles in motorsport

Antonelli wins Monaco. Hamilton quietly dangerous in red.

◍Monaco Grand Prix · Circuit de Monaco
  • Why it matters: Kimi Antonelli just won at Monaco — the tightest, least forgiving street circuit on the calendar, where one wall tap ends your afternoon. Winning there is not a fluke; it is a statement.
  • Championship: Hamilton finished second for Ferrari, and that matters. Antonelli holds a 66-point championship lead, but having your closest rival show genuine pace at the most prestigious race of the year is exactly the kind of pressure that can unravel a title campaign.
  • Watch for: Whether Ferrari can sustain this pace over the next few rounds or whether Monaco's unique demands flattered their car in a way that will not repeat on faster tracks.
  • What to say: Antonelli is running away with this championship, but Hamilton just showed up at Monaco in a Ferrari with real pace. That lead is not as comfortable as it looks on paper.

Final Sharp Take

The Spurs stole Game 3 at Madison Square Garden and cut New York's lead to 2-1, and the way they did it should concern the Knicks. San Antonio did not need to shoot the lights out — they got 23 from Castle, 13 and 9 from Harper off the bench, and Wembanyama ran the offense as much as he dominated it. Tomorrow's Game 4 is as close to a must-win as it gets for New York; dropping it at home and heading back to San Antonio tied at 2-2 turns this into a completely different series. The Knicks are still in control, but they cannot afford another night where Brunson has to carry the whole thing alone.
