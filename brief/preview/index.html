San Antonio won 115-111 at home with Victor Wembanyama matching Jalen Brunson point for point at 32 apiece, cutting New York's series lead to 2-1. The Spurs executed down the stretch while the Knicks' supporting cast couldn't keep pace, forcing a Game 4 tomorrow night in New York.

  • Why it matters: Wembanyama finished with 32 points, 8 rebounds, and 6 assists — that kind of two-way performance is exactly what San Antonio's rebuild is supposed to look like, and he delivered it on the Finals stage.
  • The other angle: The Spurs got 23 points from Stephon Castle and 13 from Dylan Harper while New York leaned heavily on OG Anunoby's 28 — San Antonio's depth held up when it mattered most, and that changes the calculus heading into Game 4.
  • What to say: Wembanyama went toe-to-toe with Brunson and the Spurs won the game anyway — New York didn't blow it, San Antonio just took it. That's a different kind of problem for the Knicks heading home tomorrow.

    He-Man movie tanks hard at the box office

    What happened: The new He-Man film landed as one of 2026's biggest box office bombs, according to early tracking.

    Why it matters: Hollywood keeps reaching back to legacy IP and betting that nostalgia alone will fill seats — this is another data point that it does not work without a story people actually want to see.

    What to say: He-Man is one of 2026's biggest box office bombs already — at some point Hollywood has to figure out that owning the IP is not the same as knowing what to do with it.

  • Sports Biz
    NBA Finals ticket prices crash after Trump attendance news

    What happened: Ticket prices for the NBA Finals dropped sharply after Donald Trump confirmed he would be attending, according to reporting on secondary market data.

    Why it matters: It is a real-time signal of how politically charged the NBA's audience has become — fans are making purchase decisions based on who else is in the building, not just who is on the court.

    What to say: NBA Finals tickets are actually getting cheaper because Trump said he's going — fans are voting with their wallets on who they want sitting courtside, and the secondary market moved fast.
