Victor Wembanyama and Jalen Brunson matched each other at 32 points apiece, but San Antonio's depth decided the game. The Spurs beat the Knicks 115-111 to cut the series to 2-1, with Stephon Castle adding 23 points and Dylan Harper chipping in 13 points and 9 rebounds when the Knicks needed stops most.

  • Why it matters: The Knicks got 28 from OG Anunoby and a solid all-around night from Josh Hart, but New York couldn't get enough from a third contributor — and that's exactly how you drop a home Finals game.
  • The other angle: San Antonio's role players outplayed New York's in the margins, which means the Knicks can no longer count on Brunson and Anunoby to carry this series alone.
  • What to say: Brunson and Wembanyama are canceling each other out, but the Spurs have Castle and Harper stepping up while the Knicks are waiting on a third guy who hasn't shown up yet. That's the actual problem for New York.

Formula 1
Monaco Grand Prix · Circuit de Monaco · 78 laps through the streets of Monte Carlo · The most iconic 3.3 miles in motorsport

Antonelli wins Monaco. Hamilton salvages podium for Ferrari.

  • Why it matters: Monaco is the most prestigious race on the calendar — it's not a normal Sunday. Winning here means something different, and Antonelli doing it this season adds another chapter to what's becoming a dominant run.
  • Championship: Hamilton finishing second for Ferrari at Monaco, a track where precision and car stability matter more than raw pace, shows the Scuderia can compete at the venues that suit them — even if the championship gap is already significant.
  • Watch for: Whether Antonelli can carry this form to high-speed circuits where Mercedes gets tested differently than on street tracks like Monaco.
  • What to say: Antonelli just won Monaco. The most glamorous race on the calendar, and he made it look routine. Hamilton kept Ferrari in the conversation but that championship gap is already a problem for everyone else.
