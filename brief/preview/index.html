Victor Wembanyama and Jalen Brunson both dropped 32 points in a back-and-forth Finals game, but San Antonio's depth showed up when it mattered. Stephon Castle added 23 points and Dylan Harper chipped in 13 points and 9 rebounds to help the Spurs pull out a 115-111 win. New York still leads the series 2-1, but San Antonio just proved they are not going quietly.

  • Why it matters: This was the first time in the series the Spurs looked like a complete team — Castle and Harper both produced, which means New York can no longer just key in on Wembanyama.
  • The other angle: OG Anunoby put up 28 points and Brunson matched Wembanyama all night, but the Knicks still lost — that is a depth problem that does not fix itself before Game 4 tomorrow.
  • What to say: The Spurs are genuinely alive. Castle and Harper both showed up in a Finals game — if that keeps happening, New York has a real problem.
