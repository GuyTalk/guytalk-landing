Victor Wembanyama and Jalen Brunson each scored 32 points in a genuine duel, but San Antonio's supporting cast made the difference. Stephon Castle added 23, Dylan Harper chipped in 13, and the Spurs walked out of New York with a 115–111 win to cut the series to 2–1.

  • Why it matters: The Knicks got 32 from Brunson and 28 from OG Anunoby and still lost at home — that means New York's role players aren't matching what San Antonio is throwing out there on any given night.
  • The other angle: Castle and Harper combined for 36 points while the Knicks' bench couldn't answer — Game 4 tomorrow is now a real test of whether New York can tighten rotations before this series gets away from them.
  • What to say: Brunson dropped 32 and still lost because Castle, Harper, and Wembanyama all showed up at the same time. The Spurs have four guys who can go off on any night — that's the actual problem for New York.

Antonelli wins Monaco. Hamilton podiums for Ferrari.

◍Monaco Grand Prix · Circuit de Monaco
  • Why it matters: Kimi Antonelli won at Monaco — the circuit where one wall tap ends your race — and made it look controlled. That is five wins this season and a championship that is starting to look like his to lose.
  • Championship: Lewis Hamilton finished second for Ferrari at the track where he built his legend driving for Mercedes. The symbolism is not lost on anyone, and it keeps the championship picture more interesting than a runaway would.
  • Watch for: Whether Red Bull can close the gap before mid-season. Isack Hadjar grabbed third, which is solid, but Mercedes is operating on a different level right now.
  • What to say: Antonelli just won Monaco and Hamilton finished second — in a Ferrari. The fact that those two sentences are both true in the same race tells you everything about how different this season looks from the last few years.

Culture
  • Sports Biz
    He-Man Movie Tanks Hard at the Box Office

    What happened: The new He-Man film landed as one of 2026's biggest box office bombs, according to early tracking.

    Why it matters: Hollywood keeps betting that every 80s IP deserves a massive budget reboot, and this one is the latest proof that nostalgia alone does not fill theaters. At some point the industry has to reckon with the fact that people who grew up with He-Man are not automatically buying tickets to see him reimagined.

    What to say: He-Man is officially one of the biggest box office bombs of the year. At some point Hollywood has to stop assuming that slapping a childhood IP on a big budget automatically works.

  • Sports Biz
    NBA Finals Tickets Crash After Trump Confirms Attendance

    What happened: NBA Finals ticket prices dropped noticeably after Donald Trump confirmed he would be attending a game, according to reporting on secondary market prices.

    Why it matters: A presidential appearance usually moves markets in the other direction — the fact that confirmed attendance sent prices down is a real data point about where the NBA fanbase stands right now.

    What to say: NBA Finals tickets actually got cheaper after Trump said he was going. That is a pretty clear signal about the vibe of the fanbase — and it is the kind of thing that will get people talking.

  • Streaming
    The Accountant 2 — Worth Your Weekend

    What happened: Ben Affleck returns in The Accountant 2, the sequel to the 2016 action thriller about a forensic accountant who is also a highly lethal contractor.

    Why it matters: It is the rare sequel that understands exactly what made the original work — precise, slightly offbeat, and built around a genuinely unusual main character rather than just bigger set pieces.

    What to say: If you liked the first Accountant, the second one actually holds up. Smart, specific, and no filler — just put it on.
