THE GUYTALK READ. World Cup

Spain 2–1 Belgium

What happened
Spain beat Belgium 2–1 yesterday, with Fabián Ruiz opening in the 30th, Charles De Ketelaere equalizing in the 41st, and Mikel Merino scoring the winner in the 88th off a rebound from Belgium backup goalkeeper Senne Lammens.
Why it matters
Spain advances in a tournament where depth at every position matters, and Belgium's reliance on a backup keeper in a critical moment exposed a real weakness. This is the kind of thing that haunts teams in knockout rounds.
Big moments
  • Merino's 88th-minute rebound goal came directly off a save from Lammens, turning a Belgium defensive moment into a Spanish winner
  • Ruiz's opening goal in the 30th gave Spain early control before De Ketelaere's 41st-minute leveler reset the match
The GuyTalk Read
Spain didn't need a perfect performance—they needed composure and a second wind, and they got both. Belgium had Belgium's chance, scored it, and then couldn't hold on when it mattered. That's a recipe for early tournament exits. Spain's midfield depth (Ruiz setting the tone, Merino finishing it) is the real story here—they can hurt you in multiple ways.
What to know
  • Fabián Ruiz scored in the 30th minute
  • Charles De Ketelaere equalized in the 41st minute
  • Mikel Merino scored the winner in the 88th minute on a rebound from backup goalkeeper Senne Lammens

Wimbledon Men's Final Set: Sinner vs. Zverev Sunday

What happened
The Wimbledon men's singles final is confirmed for Sunday: top seed Jannik Sinner, who swept Novak Djokovic 6-4, 6-4, 6-4 in Friday's semifinal, will face Alexander Zverev, who beat British wildcard Arthur Fery 7-6(0), 6-2, 6-4. Sinner is defending his title and seeking a back-to-back Wimbledon crown. Sinner-Zverev is a generational matchup — the world's two best players on grass, both in their prime, in the most prestigious tennis venue on earth. Djokovic's pursuit of a record-extending 25th Grand Slam ends here, cementing the torch-passing to the new generation.

The Scene
  • Music
    Housing bill becomes law without Trump's signature

    What happened: President Trump allowed a sweeping bipartisan housing affordability bill to become law today without signing it. Trump had withheld approval, demanding Congress first pass an elections overhaul bill that stalled among GOP lawmakers. Congress didn't blink.

    Why it matters: For men 25-45, housing costs are arguably the single biggest financial pressure point right now. This legislation directly targets inventory and pricing in overheated markets — and the fact that it passed despite Trump's resistance signals rare congressional independence on an issue that actually affects people's lives.

    The GuyTalk Read: Trump wanted leverage on elections reform and didn't get it, so the housing bill became law anyway. That's Congress saying your ability to buy a home matters more than the president's other agenda items. The bill has real teeth on supply and affordability — this isn't theater. Whether it actually moves the needle on home prices remains to be seen, but removing the political uncertainty around it is itself meaningful.

    • The bipartisan housing affordability bill passed Congress with overwhelming support
    • Trump withheld his signature, demanding Congress pass an unrelated elections overhaul bill first
    • The bill became law without a presidential signature after Trump's demand stalled
    • The legislation directly targets inventory and pricing dynamics in overheated housing markets

    What to say: The housing bill just became law — Trump didn't sign it but it passed anyway. First real shot at affordability reform in years.

  • Film
    Disney's live-action Moana panned by critics

    What happened: Disney's live-action Moana remake has been panned by critics, with reviewers calling it dismal. Dwayne Johnson reprises his role as demigod Maui, a decade after the original animated film became a massive cultural and financial hit.

    Why it matters: Disney's live-action remake strategy has been under scrutiny for years, and a critical failure on one of their most beloved recent properties is a real signal that the formula may be running out of road. The original Moana worked because of its animation and heart — translating that to live-action was always a risk.

    The GuyTalk Read: Disney keeps going back to the live-action remake well and keeps getting burned by critics, and yet the strategy persists because the box office sometimes cooperates anyway. The Rock returning as Maui was supposed to be the safe play — he's one of the most bankable stars in Hollywood. If even that isn't enough to get critics on board, it raises a real question about whether Disney knows what made the original work in the first place.

    • Critics have called Disney's live-action Moana 'dismal'
    • Dwayne Johnson reprises his role as demigod Maui, a decade after the original 2016 animated film
    • The original Moana was a major cultural and financial success for Disney

    What to say: Disney's live-action Moana is getting destroyed by critics — The Rock came back and everything, and it still didn't work.

  • Streaming
    Streaming pick: Slow Horses (Apple TV+)

    What happened: If you haven't started Slow Horses on Apple TV+, this is the weekend. It's a British spy thriller following a team of disgraced MI5 agents — think le Carré without the glamour, all bureaucratic paranoia and sharp writing.

    Why it matters: Gary Oldman is doing some of the best work of his career as the shambling, brilliant Jackson Lamb. It's the kind of show you finish an episode of and immediately start the next one.

    The GuyTalk Read: Four seasons in and it hasn't lost a step. The pacing is slow by design — it earns every payoff. Perfect Saturday night watch if the World Cup games run long.

    • Dwayne Johnson returns as Maui in Moana sequel
    • Original Moana released approximately 2016, making this a decade-later revival
    • Johnson is one of Hollywood's most bankable and recognizable stars

    What to say: Slow Horses on Apple TV+ — Gary Oldman, British spies, no gadgets, just great writing. Best show most people haven't started yet.
