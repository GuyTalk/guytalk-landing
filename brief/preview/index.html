San Antonio won 115-111 to cut New York's series lead to 2-1, with Victor Wembanyama and Jalen Brunson each dropping 32 points in a back-and-forth battle. Stephon Castle added 23 points and Dylan Harper chipped in 13, while OG Anunoby put up 28 for the Knicks to keep it close until the final buzzer.

  • Why it matters: New York still holds home-court advantage and leads the series, so one Spurs win in San Antonio does not flip the script — it just confirms this Finals is genuinely competitive.
  • The other angle: Wembanyama and Castle both carrying offensive loads in the same game shows San Antonio is not a one-man show, which changes how you have to defend them going into Game 4.
  • What to say: Knicks are up 2-1 and heading home tomorrow — that is the better seat. But Wembanyama dropped 32 and Castle added 23, so San Antonio just proved they can put up a fight without leaning on one guy.
