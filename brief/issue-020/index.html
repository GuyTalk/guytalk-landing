Charles Schwab Challenge: Russell Henley wins at -13.

Russell Henley won the Charles Schwab Challenge at 13-under par.

  • Leaderboard (Final):Russell Henley -13 (1st), Eric Cole -12 (2nd), Ben Griffin -11 (3rd), Alex Smalley -11 (4th), Mac Meissner -11 (5th).
  • Why it matters:Henley's fourth PGA Tour win this season puts him squarely in major contention heading into the U.S. Open at Pinehurst in two weeks, and he's locked into the FedEx Cup playoffs conversation with real momentum.
  • How it happened:Henley finished 13-under at Colonial, holding off Eric Cole who carded 12-under. A three-way tie for third between Griffin, Smalley, and Meissner at 11-under meant the back nine separated the field—Henley's ball-striking down the stretch closed the deal.
  • What to bring up:Colonial's rough is historically punishing, but Henley's iron play through the middle rounds kept him out of it entirely. That's a skill that travels to Pinehurst's native rough.
