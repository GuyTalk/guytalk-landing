THE GUYTALK TAKE

Our Honest Read

We've handed this stick to rookies and veterans alike, and it plays both roles like a pro.

The Arturo Fuente Hemingway Short Story delivers that rare Cadillac-level build in a 45-minute package—no aging required, no apologies.

It’s got Cameroon sweetness and Dominican backbone, plus that effortless burn that makes you look good, not like you’re trying too hard.

Sure, some batches wobble, but when it hits, it's textbook consistency.

WHY WE RECOMMEND IT

The Case For It

  • It's a premium-made perfecto that's hard to roll well yet consistently performs.
  • Cameroon wrapper adds nuanced sweetness—think sugar‑cookie, cedar and raisin—not just straight tobacco.
  • At around $9, it’s absurd value for a reliable, complex smoke under an hour.
  • Its short format makes it ideal for group settings—or when you want quality without a two‑hour commitment.

THE BRAND

Who Makes This

Arturo Fuente is a family‑owned Dominican icon—started post‑Depression, rebuilt after a fire, now known for craftsmanship over hype.

The Hemingway line launched in 1983 as a tribute to Papa himself and evolved into a standard‑bearer.

Fuente doesn’t chase trends—they stick to quality, classic blends, and hand‑rolling virtuosity.

HOW TO USE IT

Making It Work

Clip the head cleanly, toast the closed foot evenly—no rush to soak the whole foot in flame.

Puff deliberately (about one draw per minute) to stretch the smoke to a clean 45 minutes.

Pair with a wood‑forward bourbon or lighter spirit so the candy‑like Cameroon doesn’t vanish.
