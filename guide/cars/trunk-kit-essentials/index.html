The Trunk Kit — What Every Guy Should Carry

Five items under $300 total that turn a car owner into a car guy. No car knowledge required.

The Philosophy

Being a car guy isn't about knowing how to rebuild an engine. It's about being prepared. The guy who has a jump pack when someone needs one, who knows his car is on record if there's an accident, who shows up with a clean car to a function — that's quiet competence, and it signals the same thing in the parking lot that a good watch signals at dinner. You don't have to be a mechanic. You have to have your trunk together.

The Five Items
  • NOCO Boost Plus GB40 (~$100) — A lithium jump pack that starts a dead battery in seconds without another car. Compact enough to live in a glovebox. Doubles as a phone charger. Buy it before you need it — you will need it.
  • VIOFO A139 Dash Cam (~$130) — Records front and rear continuously. The first time it settles an accident dispute, it pays for itself a hundred times over. No subscription required. Set it, forget it.
  • Chemical Guys Wash Kit (~$60) — Two-bucket method soap, wash mitt, drying towels, detail spray. A properly washed car looks better than a new car with six months of drive-throughs on it. This is the setup to do it right.
  • WeatherTech FloorLiners (~$180) — Laser-fit mats that protect your carpet from salt, mud, coffee, and every other thing your life puts on your floors. Preserves resale value and keeps the interior looking new for years.
  • Leather Honey Conditioner (~$20) — Two applications a year keeps leather seats from cracking, fading, or feeling old. Same bottle works on boots, bags, and belts. Highest return on investment of any car product you'll buy.
