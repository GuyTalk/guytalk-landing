Courses Every Guy Should Know

Where to play & what to play. From $15 public 9-holes to the courses that live in golf lore.

Not all golf courses are equal. The difference between a par-3 executive course, a public 18-hole, and a private club is massive—in cost, difficulty, and experience. Where you play communicates something about your skill level and your access. Know the hierarchy.

Where to Start (9-Hole Executive Courses)

Best for: Learning to play or warming up your game. These are short (par-3 and par-4 holes only), usually take 90 minutes, and cost $10-20. They're not "training wheels"—they're practical.
Three to know:
  • Local par-3 courses — Every major city has one. Designed for beginners and weekend players. No shame. Everyone starts here.
  • Executive 9-holes — Slightly longer than par-3, better maintained. Good for practicing without the intensity of 18 holes.
  • Best budget option — Find your local municipal course's 9-hole offering. Usually $12-18 on weekends.

The Public 18-Hole Standard

Once you can consistently shoot 90+, you're ready for 18 holes. Public courses (anyone can play, no membership required) cost $25-50 depending on location and course quality.
Courses every guy should play:
  • TPC Scottsdale (Arizona) — Where the PGA Tour plays. Public access to some holes. Intimidating but bucket-list.
  • Torrey Pines (California) — Stunning oceanside course near San Diego. Public play available. $100-130 but worth it once.
  • Pinehurst No. 2 (North Carolina) — Historic and beautiful. Not the hardest but one of the most iconic in America. $150-200.
  • Local muni courses — Your city has good ones. Find them. They're where 90% of real golf happens.

Private Clubs (The Long Game)

Private clubs require membership ($1,000-$30,000+ annually). You don't need one to be a good golfer, but they offer consistency and access. Some require introductions or sponsorships. If you're serious about golf (playing 30+ times a year), a private club eventually makes sense.

Course Difficulty & What You Should Play

  • If you're shooting 100+: Play 9-holes or super easy par-3 courses
  • If you're shooting 90-100: Public 18-holes at easy/medium difficulty
  • If you're shooting 80-90: Mid-difficulty public courses, some private clubs
  • If you're shooting under 80: You already know where to play

Key Takeaways

  • Start at 9-hole par-3 courses; no shame and perfect for learning
  • Public 18-holes are $25-50; they're where most real golf happens
  • TPC Scottsdale, Torrey Pines, Pinehurst are bucket-list rounds
  • Private club membership makes sense at 30+ rounds per year
  • Match course difficulty to your skill level; playing over your head is miserable
