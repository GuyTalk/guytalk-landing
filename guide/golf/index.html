The Four Things That Actually Matter
Ball. Rangefinder. Bag. Driver. Everything else is noise until you have these.

Golf has a gear problem — every brand convinces you that you need the latest driver, five wedges, and a $600 bag before you can show up. You don't. What separates a guy who looks like he plays from a guy who clearly doesn't is simpler: a quality ball that matches your swing speed, a rangefinder so you stop guessing yardages, a clean stand bag that isn't falling apart, and one driver you've actually hit more than twice. The Pro V1 is the standard because it is the standard. The Bushnell Pro X3 gives you yardages in two seconds and never embarrasses you on the course. Vessel bags look like they belong at a member's club. Get these right first — then obsess over irons.
