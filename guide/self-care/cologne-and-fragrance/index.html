Cologne & Fragrance

Application, timing, and not drowning in scent. Wear it right.

Application Basics

Spray on skin, not clothes. 2-3 spritzes maximum. Pulse points: wrists, base of neck, inside elbows. Wait 3 seconds between sprays so they don't just mix into one cloud. The goal is that people smell you when they're close, not from across the room.

Timing Matters

Apply cologne right after showering when your skin is still warm and slightly damp. Heat releases scent better than cold skin. Apply after moisturizer and sunscreen (cologne sits better on hydrated skin) but before getting dressed.

Common Mistakes

  • Spraying and walking through the cloud — You're just wasting it and drowning everything
  • Applying to clothes — Cologne fades faster and stains lighter fabrics
  • Reapplying throughout the day — Your nose goes blind to the scent around hour 2-3, so you think it's gone. It's not. Most people can still smell you.
  • Wearing the same fragrance every day — Variety matters. Having 2-3 go-to scents is better than rotating through 10.

Choosing Your Scents

Three categories covers 95% of situations:
Everyday — Something fresh that works morning-to-afternoon. Doesn't project too much. Brands: Sauvage, Bleu de Chanel, Acqua di Parma.
Work/Office — Clean and professional. Nothing too spicy or sweet. Brands: Heeley Sel Marin, Hermès Eau de Gentiane Blanche.
Night/Going Out — You can go darker or more complex. Projection can be higher. Brands: Tom Ford Tobacco Vanille, Dior Homme Intense, Givenchy Gentleman Reserve Privée.
Don't need all three, but one everyday + one for nights out covers 95% of situations.

Key Takeaways

  • 2-3 spritzes on skin (wrists, neck, inside elbows), not clothes
  • Apply right after shower on still-warm skin
  • You go nose-blind; others still smell it—don't re-apply
  • One good everyday scent + one for nights out is all you need
  • Rotation > obsessive collecting; wear the same few fragrances consistently
