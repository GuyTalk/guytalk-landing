## Week 2 — Ship the landing page (8-12 hrs)

**The goal:** one sharp page that captures emails. Nothing more.

- [ ] **Stack recommendation:** Next.js + Vercel (free) + Tailwind. Or for fastest path: Framer / Carrd if you want to skip the code.
- [ ] **Page sections (in order):**
  1. Hero: "Sports. Markets. Culture. In five minutes." + email capture
  2. The pitch: 3 bullet points on what makes GuyTalk different
  3. Sample brief (mock screenshot of the cards from the brand preview)
  4. Founder note (your face, 2 paragraphs, why you're building this — builds trust)
  5. Footer with social + email
- [ ] **Email capture:** ConvertKit, Beehiiv, or Resend. Beehiiv has built-in newsletter tooling so you don't have to migrate later — recommended.
- [ ] **Analytics:** Plausible or Vercel Analytics. Skip Google Analytics for now.

---

## Week 3 — Write & ship the first 3 newsletter issues (8-12 hrs)

**The goal:** prove the product to yourself before launching publicly. Write three full issues, even if no one reads them yet.

- [ ] Pick your two anchor verticals to start: **Sports + Markets** is the safest combo. Add Culture as a "Worth Watching" section.
- [ ] **Format template (~5 min read):**
  - "Top 3" — three things to know across all verticals
  - Markets section — 2 short stories, 1 ticker rundown
  - Sports section — 2 short stories, scores/standings
  - "Around the Internet" — 3 culture/trending links
  - "Sharp Take" — one short editorial paragraph in GuyTalk voice
- [ ] Write three issues. Don't publish yet. Send them to 5 friends in the target demo and ask what they'd change.

---

## Week 4 — Soft launch (5-8 hrs)

**The goal:** 100 signups from your personal network. Real validation.

- [ ] **Publish the landing page.**
- [ ] **Personal outreach** — text/DM 50-100 people in your demo. Not a mass post — individual messages. "Hey, building this thing for guys 18-30, would love your eyes on it: [link]"
- [ ] **First public post** on your own social — short, founder voice, link.
- [ ] **Launch on Product Hunt? No.** Too early. ProductHunt is for products, not waitlists.
- [ ] **Start the email cadence** — even with 10 signups, send a weekly brief. Frequency matters more than audience size right now.

---

## What NOT to do in the next 30 days

- ❌ Don't build the app yet. The newsletter IS the MVP.
- ❌ Don't hire designers or contractors. You can ship this solo.
- ❌ Don't run paid ads. Zero ROI until you've validated content.
- ❌ Don't worry about logo perfection. The wordmark from the brand preview is enough.
- ❌ Don't post-and-pray on TikTok with no content strategy. Wait until you have ~5 issues out and a real POV.

---

## 90-Day Horizon (after the 30-day plan)

- **Month 2:** Settle into 2-3x/week newsletter cadence. Hit 500 subs. Start IG content (carousel summaries of each issue).
- **Month 3:** First brand partnership or affiliate deal. Hit 1,500 subs. Decide if app is a v2 priority or if you double down on media first.

The honest move: most "media brand → app" plays die because they try to build the app too early. Get to 5,000 newsletter subs and a real audience before writing a line of app code.
