## 1. The One-Liner

**GuyTalk is the daily brief for young men who want to feel sharp on sports, markets, and culture — without losing hours to the feed.**

Think: if Morning Brew, ESPN, and GQ had a kid raised on Linear and Robinhood.

---

## 2. Positioning Statement

For young men (18–30) who want to feel informed and in-the-know without spending hours scrolling, **GuyTalk** is a clean, fast daily brief on the topics that actually come up in their lives — sports, markets, culture, fitness, style, and trending conversations.

Unlike Morning Brew (too finance-bro), Barstool (too crude), or social feeds (too noisy), GuyTalk respects your time and your intelligence.

---

## 3. Audience

**Primary:** Men 18–30. Two overlapping sub-segments:

- **College guys** — frats, athletes, business/econ majors. Care about social signaling, what their friends are talking about, looking sharp.
- **Young professionals** — first jobs in NYC, Miami, Austin, Chicago, LA. Want to sound smart at work, at the gym, at the bar. Have disposable income.

Both share: scrolling X / IG / TikTok for hours, never feel caught up, hate how chaotic it feels.

---

## 4. Brand Pillars

1. **Informed** — substance over hype. We don't chase clickbait.
2. **Sharp** — every word, pixel, and design choice feels premium.
3. **Fast** — five minutes is the unit of value. Respect the user's time.
4. **Social** — built to make you the smartest guy in the group chat.
5. **Modern** — feels like a 2026 product, not a 2010 magazine.

---

## 5. Voice & Tone

**We sound like:** a sharp friend who reads everything, calls it like he sees it, and never wastes your time.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Confident | Cocky |
| Witty | Goofy |
| Smart | Academic |
| Punchy and short | Long and explainy |
| Active voice | Passive voice |
| "Markets ripped." | "Markets experienced significant gains." |
| Reference culture naturally | Force memes / try too hard |

**Sentence rhythm:** short. Then medium. Then short again. Mix it up. Never let a sentence breathe past 20 words unless it has to.

---

## 6. What GuyTalk Is NOT

- ❌ Not Barstool — we're not crude, mean, or punching down
- ❌ Not "alpha male" / Tate / Rogan-adjacent — no grindset, no cringe
- ❌ Not a Morning Brew clone — we're broader and less finance-coded
- ❌ Not Men's Health — we're not a magazine, we're a product
- ❌ Not GQ — we're not luxury aspirational, we're daily utility
- ❌ Not a Substack — we're a brand and an app, not one writer's voice

## 7. Visual Identity

### Color System

| Role | HEX | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Background | `#0B1322` | Primary app/site background — deep navy-black with subtle blue undertone |
| Surface | `#131D30` | Cards, modals, elevated panels |
| Surface Elevated | `#1A2640` | Hover states, dropdowns, secondary surfaces |
| Border | `#1F2B43` | Dividers, input outlines |
| Text Primary | `#F2F4F7` | Headlines and body |
| Text Secondary | `#8B96AB` | Metadata, captions, timestamps |
| Accent Blue | `#3D7EFF` | CTAs, links, key callouts, brand mark |
| Accent Blue (hover) | `#5B95FF` | Interactive states |
| Accent Muted | `#1E3A6B` | Subtle highlights, accent backgrounds |

Dark-mode first. Light mode is a future concern, not v1.

### Typography

- **Display / Headlines:** Inter Tight (Bold / Semibold) — modern, tight, premium
- **Body:** Inter (Regular / Medium) — workhorse, legible at every size
- **Numbers / Tickers / Labels:** JetBrains Mono — gives the finance-app feel

All free via Google Fonts. No licensing headaches.

### Logo Direction (v1) — LOCKED

**Final wordmark:** `GuyTalk.` — sentence case, Inter Tight Extrabold (800), with a blue accent period (`#3D7EFF`) at the end.

- Set in Inter Tight 800, tight tracking (-0.03em)
- The blue period is a signature element — it should appear everywhere the wordmark does
- **App icon:** a bold `g.` monogram in a deep navy squared tile with the accent blue period

### Design Principles

1. **Negative space is a feature.** Don't fill it.
2. **One accent color.** Resist the urge for a rainbow.
3. **Type does the heavy lifting.** Hierarchy through size and weight, not color.
4. **Borders > shadows.** Hairline `#22272E` borders, not drop shadows.
5. **Numbers feel like data.** Mono font for any stat, ticker, or score.
