# GuyTalk — Product Vision

*A living doc of where GuyTalk is going beyond the MVP newsletter. Not a build plan yet — just keeping track of the bigger picture so the brand foundation can hint at it.*

---

## The Long View

GuyTalk is **not just a newsletter.** It's a media brand and product that becomes:

- A daily brief (the MVP, content-first)
- A native app with real-time, contextual notifications
- A social layer where users contribute, react, and shape the conversation
- The trusted "what's happening" hub for young men 18–30

The newsletter is the wedge. The app and community are the destination.

---

## Future Features (logged, not committed)

### "Before You Go Out" Notification ⭐
*Jake's idea — May 16, 2026*

A smart push notification that fires Thursday–Saturday evenings (roughly 5–7 PM local) with the rundown of what's worth knowing for the night ahead:

- Big games tipping off tonight + the stakes
- Star players to watch, storylines, betting lines (if relevant)
- Trending topics dominating group chats
- One sharp culture/news item worth bringing up

**Why it's killer:** it solves a real moment — the "I'm about to be around people, what do I need to know" gut check. Nothing else nails this. Bleacher Report is too broad. ESPN notifications are dumb pings. Group chats are chaos. GuyTalk = your pre-game brief.

**Format ideas:**
- Push notification → opens to a 60-second scan screen in the app
- Headline + 3-4 bullets + "Talking points" section
- Maybe an audio version (90-second voice memo) for guys driving / getting ready

### Social / Community Layer
*Jake noted as a "soon" priority*

- User reactions / hot-takes on briefs
- A "your take" feature where users submit content/opinions
- Light social graph: follow your buddies, see what they're reacting to
- NOT trying to be Twitter or Reddit — closer to a "group chat for the GuyTalk community"

### Other ideas to circle back to

- **Personalized brief** — pick your teams, your stocks, your interests
- **Audio briefs** (60-90 sec, voice-driven, for commutes)
- **Live game threads / event coverage** during big moments
- **Brand partnerships / commerce** — recommended gear, drops, etc.

---

## What this means for the brand foundation

The landing page should **hint at the bigger product** without overpromising. Specifically:

- Position the brief as the entry point, not the whole thing
- CTA should feel like "join the platform" not "subscribe to the newsletter"
- Visuals should suggest an app is coming (mobile mockup eventually, push notification mock)
- Voice should feel like a product, not a writer with a column

That's the line we're walking: substance of a newsletter, identity of a modern app.
