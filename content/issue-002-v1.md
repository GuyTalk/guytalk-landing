## Markets

**The week in one line:** Monday opened ugly. Tech, bonds, oil — all moving against each other for the wrong reasons.

### Bond yields blew the top off.

The [10-year Treasury yield](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/US10Y) closed at its highest level in a year. When the long end of the curve rips like that, growth stocks get re-priced — fast.

- **S&P 500:** −1.24% to 7,408.50
- **Nasdaq:** −1.51% to 26,225.15
- **Dow:** −1.07% to 49,526.17
- **Why it matters:** This is the first −1% day on the S&P in over a month. Six straight up weeks have made everyone forget what a sell-off looks like. Monday was the reminder.
- **The angle nobody's talking about:** Tech got punched twice as hard as the broader index. [S&P Tech Sector](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/equity/sp-500-information-technology-sector/) was down 2.3% intraday before clawing back. Translation: when yields move, mega-cap tech moves more. Always has, always will.

### Oil at $108. Iran is back.

The [US–Iran](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/) situation is the only macro story that matters this week. Oil cleared $108 on the spot market Monday. Gas-prices talk is officially re-entering the political discourse — Trump said in a Fox interview he expects prices to "drop like a rock" once the dust settles.

- **What to bring up:** "Every penny on gas is roughly $1.4B a year out of consumer spending. Track the front-month [crude futures](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@CL.1) — that's the leading indicator for whether you should care about inflation next month."

### Crypto, gold, silver — all hit.

- **[Bitcoin](https://www.coingecko.com/en/coins/bitcoin):** Pulled back to ~$77k from a recent run above $80k. Risk-off Monday hit everything.
- **Gold + silver:** Both down despite the geopolitical noise. Strong dollar, rising real yields — gold's worst environment.

## Sharp Take

If you want one thing to take away from the last 72 hours of basketball — it's this. Game 7 doesn't tell you who the better team was. It tells you who *showed up.* The Cavs as a 4 seed walked into Detroit and turned the 1 seed into a 31-point road kill. The Knicks waiting to host them tonight are wondering what a Cleveland team *not* in survival mode looks like — they're about to find out. Half of the second season is matchup. The other half is psychology. Cleveland just won the second half.

## Numbers worth stealing.

01 — **31.** Cleveland's Game 7 margin of victory. Game 7s with a 30+ point margin since 1985: six. Two of them belong to LeBron-era Cavs squads.

02 — **+18 bps.** This week's move in the 10-year Treasury. When the long bond moves more than 15 bps in a week, the Nasdaq closes red 73% of the time the next 5 sessions. That's a base rate, not a prediction.

03 — **62 wins.** The 2025–26 Spurs' regular-season total. That's a +28 swing from two seasons ago. The only team in NBA history with a bigger two-year regular-season swing: the 1989–90 Spurs. Same uniform, different era. Wemby is the variable both times share.
